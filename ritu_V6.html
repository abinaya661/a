A Note Before We Begin
Letter from Abi.
for Ritu, on her 21st

There are people who fill a room with noise. And then there are people like you — who walk in and quietly make everything warmer. You have never once tried to be the most important person in the room, and yet somehow, you always are.

I have watched you carry more than you should — everyone else's feelings, the weight of expectations, the habit of making yourself smaller in rooms where you deserve to take up all the space. You apologise first. You share before you taste. You stay on the call until 1am because someone needed you. And you do it all without making it a thing.

This magazine is my love letter to the real version of you — every honest answer, every 2am thought, every wrong lyric you've sung at full confidence, every dream you hold quietly. You deserve to be seen, fully and completely. So this year, I put it all on paper.

Welcome to your era, Ritu.

with all my love, Abi 🤍

The Rebrand · Who She Is Becoming
Her
New Era.
She is not the same girl she was last year. She knows it. You can see it. This is what it looks like up close.

She carried things quietly. Fear of judgement, worn like a second skin. People-pleasing, disguised as kindness. Anxiety that knocked the wind out of her before she could even begin. She made herself smaller in rooms where she deserved to take up the most space. She was extraordinary, and she spent most of the year believing she was barely enough.

Now

She is studying harder than she ever has — not because she has to, but because she wants to. She is holding her own opinions with both hands. She is choosing, consciously and daily, to leave people-pleasing behind like a coat that never quite fit. She is building the confidence that was always hers to have. She is arriving, one small brave day at a time, at herself.

"What she is leaving behind: fear of judgement, the need to be needed, the habit of shrinking.
What she is stepping into: herself."
— Abi, watching it happen
What Actually Changed
She used to say sorry before she finished her sentence. She used to make herself quieter in rooms where she deserved to be the loudest. This year, something shifted. She started studying because she genuinely wanted to. She started speaking up in conversations instead of editing herself first. Small things — but anyone who knows her can see it. She is not the same girl she was at 20. She is better. More hers.
